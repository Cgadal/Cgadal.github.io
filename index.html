Hi!
I am a Postdoctral researcher at the Institut de Mécanique des Fluides de Toulouse, driving experiments on turbidity currents.

Before, I was doing my PhD on sand dunes at the Institut de physique du globe de Paris. My main research interests are:
  • instabilities, patterns resulting from different couplings (fluid/grains, fluid/chemical reactions)
  • granular flows, sediment transport, suspensions (dunes, turbidity currents)
  • quantitative analysis of landscape morphodynamics
  • open research: distribution of analysis codes/research data (F. A. I. R.)

Research

During my PhD, my research mainly focus on aeolian dunes and sedimentary bedforms. Using a combination of laboratory experiments and numerical simulations, together with field data, we aim to develop a physical understanding of the mechanisms acting in dune morphodynamics. The resulting theoretical framework is then used to reliably interpret field data, and draw conclusions on the development of sand seas and sedimentary bassins over climatic cycles. We are also able to infer or constrain sediment and wind properties from dune characteristics in places where in situ measurements are not possible or available.
